Before this project, I viewed the internet mostly as a consumer. Setting up a GitHub repository and a LinkedIn profile made me realize that I am now a creator. Seeing my name on a professional platform like HackerRank was a turning point—it made me feel like an engineer in training rather than just a student in a classroom.

The Learning Curve
The most humbling part was the coding task. I spent nearly half an hour debugging a simple Python script only to find a tiny spelling mistake in a string. It taught me that while AI and tools are great, "Digital Literacy" also means having the patience and eye for detail to fix your own mistakes. It wasn't about the code; it was about the discipline of being precise.

Real-World Impact
The Cybercrime module (Task 5) had an immediate impact on my personal life. I realized that even as "Tech-Savvy" students, we are often overconfident. After learning about the UPI "Request Money" scams, I actually sat down with my parents to explain why they should never enter a PIN to receive a prize. It felt good to use my college assignments to keep my family safe.

Professional Growth
I used to think email was "just for old people," but drafting the extension request and internship inquiry made me see it as a tool for respect. I realized that a missing subject line or a casual "hey" can close doors before I even get a chance to show my skills. Moving forward, I’m committing to a "Think Before You Post" habit to ensure my digital footprint helps my career instead of hurting it.
